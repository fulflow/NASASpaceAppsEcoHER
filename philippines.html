Labor Burden

Extreme weather events such as typhoons and floods have a greater impact on women due to their traditional roles as caregivers and food providers.

Solutions for Labor Burden

Upgrading Flood Defenses: - Sea Walls and Levees: Coastal and low-lying regions in the Philippines are highly susceptible to typhoon surges and rising sea levels. Reinforcing existing sea walls and building new levees can help protect coastal cities like Manila, Tacloban, and Cebu. These structures act as physical barriers to rising waters and prevent flooding from typhoons or storm surges.- Elevated Roads and Bridges: Roads and bridges in flood-prone areas need to be elevated to prevent them from being submerged during heavy rains or typhoons, ensuring connectivity during disasters.

Green infrastructure: Implement nature-based solutions like planting mangroves, restoring wetlands, and creating urban green spaces to absorb floodwater.

Existing Solutions: The Magna Carta of Women (2009) ensures women‚Äôs rights to protection during crises, and the Climate Change Act (2009) adopts a gender-sensitive approach to tackling climate challenges. These policies aim to create a social safety net for communities, anticipating future risks through proactive planning and gender-responsive strategies.

Limited Access to Resources

There's not much developed resources here for women! They have limited access to resources such as land, credit and technology, which makes it more difficult to adapt to the changing climate. Women in Southeast Asia, especially in rural areas, perform much of the agricultural labor but seldom own lands or tools.

Solutions for Limited Resource Accessibility

Implement Disaster Preparedness & Response Programs: For example, The Global Precipitation Measurement (GPM) mission provides real-time rainfall data, which can help predict the intensity of upcoming storms and mitigate flood risks. This data can be used for early warning systems to predict typhoons, floods, and droughts, giving communities time to prepare.

Improve drainage systems: Develop better urban drainage systems and maintain existing ones to reduce water buildup during heavy rains.

Women-Centered Relief Programs: Ensuring that women, particularly caregivers and heads of households, are provided with the resources they need during disasters is essential. Women should have access to safe spaces in shelters, hygiene products, and childcare.

Existing Solutions: Sigay Nu Mga Babay, a women-led organization in Maguindanao, Philippines, implemented community gardens to address food shortages during flooding, demonstrating how gender-responsive actions can be effective.
